Models

The Triumph Spitfire is a two-seater sports car with a four-cylinder in-line engine, built between 1962 to 1980 by the British car manufacturer Triumph , designed by Harry Webster, and equipped with a body designed by Giovanni Michelotti. The Spitfire was manufactured at the Standard-Triumph works at Canley, in Coventry UK.

The bodywork was fitted to a separate structural chassis, but for the open-top convertible Spitfire the backbone chassis' rigidity was augmented by the use of structural components within the bodywork, with the rear trailing arms being bolted to the body rather than the chassis.

Spitfire 4 or MKI (1962 - 1964)

The production car changed little from the prototype, although the full-width rear bumper was dropped in favour of two part-bumpers curving around each corner, with overriders. The mechanicals were basically from a stock Herald with the notable addition of front disc brakes. The engine was an 1,147 cc four-cylinder with a pushrod OHV cylinder head and two valves per cylinder, mildly tuned for the Spitfire, fed by twin SU carburettors.

Spitfire MKII (1964 - 1967)

Introduction of the Spitfire MK2 with following changes: engine power was increased from 63 BHP to 67 BHP, primarily achieved by a new camshaft and a tubular manifold. Aluminium fan blade fitted. Oil filler cap no longer used for ventilation. The Delco Remy D200 type distributor (serial no. 7953166) got a new vacuum unit and different centrifugal advance weights. The coil spring clutch was replaced by a modern diaphragm spring clutch. Revised radiator grille with horizontal slats. New badges fitted. New water temperature gauge with continuous scale and new tachometer. Twin black air filter box. Choke knob no longer removable from cable. Redesigned interior grab handle. Seats now had piping and better padding. Paint and trim codes added to commission plate.

Spitfire MKIII (1967 - 1970)

Introduction of the Spitfire MK3 with following changes: new 1300 cc. engine with 8 port cylinder head. The power was up from 67 BHP to 75 BPH. A cast exhaust manifold replaced the tubular manifold used on the MK2. New 4-port water heated inlet manifold with engine breathing control valve. Narrower radiator with fan guard. New 4 bladed fan (aluminium early, yellow painted steel later) for all countries except Germany. Exports for Germany fitted with a 6 bladed fan. New 3-synchro 3-rail gearbox, identified by the number prefix: FD and loose mounted needle bearing between first motion shaft and main shaft. Fusebox now mounted to bulkhead. Car electric now with negitive earth. Body modifications: the front bumpers raised by 9“ changes to the front indicator lamps to suit. The rear bumpers raised and the overriders were deleted. Rear indicator lenses now plastic. Two reverse lamps now standard. Redesigned hood frame. New bonnet, now featured with a wider aluminium radiator grille. Wire wheels and a redesigned hardtop are now available as special order. Interior modifications: 15“ wire spoked steering wheel, wood veneer dashboard, positions of ignition switch and light switch swapped. New 120 MPH resp. 180 KMH speedometer. Positions of ignition switch and light switch swapped. Ignition switch and lock now standard for export models to Germany. Breakaway interior mirror. Seat belt anchors added. Indicator stick cowls go from metal to plastic. The round gear knob was now inscribed with the positions of the gears. Dual line brake system introduced to all LHD markets except Belgium, France, Luxembourg and the Netherlands. Accordingly new brake master cylinder and pressure failure switch fitted to suit.

Spitfire MKIV (1970 - 1974)

Introduction of the Spitfire MK4 with a new style based on that of the Triumph Stag: a new bonnet, a new front bumper with black plastic underriders, a new radiator grille, a new windscreen frame integrally pressed with the scuttle top, new door shells, new rear wings with decking panel, rear valance and boot lid with lock, new full-width wrap-around rear bumper. Engine modification: stronger push rods, new crankshaft - identify by 1.3/8“ diameter nose, new cylinder block to accommodate the larger bearings and con rods, detail changes on front engine plate and tming cover to suit. The crank case breathing system changed again and with it the rocker cover. The net result of the changes to the engine was a power of 63 DIN PS on European models. Further modification: new rear lamps. New 4.1/2Jx13“ disc wheels. New rear suspension arrangement with a new spring with five leaves instead of six. Larger diameter flanges on gearbox and differential. Rear axle with a higher ratio of 3.89:1 for more relaxed high speed driving. New 4-synchro 3-rail gearbox, identify by the number prefix: FH. The gear knob changed from ball to conical shape. The bulkhead behind the battery now included a recess to adapt the dual line brake master cylinder fitted to LHD models. New outer door handle with integrated lock barrel. A new air filter box was introduced. A new seven blade plastic fan was fitted. New radiator cap with 13 lbs pressure. For the first time the carburettors were cable-operated and a new accelerator linkage and bracket for the throttle return springs were bolted to the inlet manifold. An ignition ballast resistor was introduced for improved cold starting. New 14W type Lucas 2-speed wiper motor fitted. Horn relay fitted to either battery box or bulkhead. A new electric windscreen washer pump was fitted to all Swedish models. New Delco Remy D204 type distributor with reference no. 7953460. An alternator, supplied by either Lucas or AC Delco was fitted as standard. A square Lucas 4ST starter solenoid was fitted now as standard. Also the speedometer and tachometer cables now no longer passed through the heater unit. Because of a different arrangement of heater controls on the dashboard, a new heater valve was used. New grain finished black plastic dashboard with new Jaeger instruments. Wiper switch incorporated pump-action washer. High beam switch moved to the steering column. Ignition switch and lock now standard in all countries. Redesigned seats with slide handles coming out from under the front of the seat and long tilt handle at rear. Seat belts now standard equipment.

Spitfire 1500 (1974 - 1980)

The Spitfire 1500 was introduced to all European markets with following modifications: new 1500 cc. engine with a compression ratio of 9:1 and 71 DIN PS. Red rocker cover with chrome filler cap. Full flow oil filter fitted. Larger 7.25“ clutch with 20 splines to suit to the new 4-synchro single-rail gearbox, identify by the number prefix: FR. New Lucas 45D4 type distributor fitted. Electric tachometer. Rear axle with a higher ratio of 3.63:1. New twin SU-HS4 type carburettors, reference no: AUD665. Heat shield between manifolds. "4-2-1" exhaust with cut out in frame for clearance. Larger 7-bladed fan made from plastic. Interior light in boot now standard, Sill plates fitted. Commission plates had "Spitfire 1500" at top. "Spitfire 1500" decals on bonnet and boot lid. Silver hub caps. Black boot trim mouldings at rear. New seats fitted.
